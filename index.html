The voice of the people and the soul of Ukraine!

Taras Grigorovich Shevchenko is a Ukrainian poet, novelist, thinker, painter, engraver, ethnographer, public figure. National hero and symbol of Ukraine.

Lifetime

Taras Shevchenko is a symbol of Ukrainian culture and the struggle for freedom. Born a serf, he rose from being a disenfranchised servant to becoming a world-renowned poet, artist, and thinker.

Ransomed from slavery, Shevchenko created brilliant works that became the soul of the Ukrainian nation, but he was exiled and persecuted for his views. His poems are a cry for justice, love for his native land, and a dream of a free Ukraine that still inspires generations. (Source)

Influence

The influence of Taras Shevchenko on Ukrainian culture and consciousness cannot be overestimated. His poetry became the voice of the people, a call to fight for freedom and national dignity. He inspired generations of artists, writers, and patriots, and his words still resonate today. (Source)

Without Shevchenko, there would be no modern Ukraine as we know it, because his ideas continue to shape the spirit of the nation and awaken the desire for independence.(Source)

Why I choce Taras Shevchenko?

Taras Shevchenko is one of the greatest masters of Ukrainian fine art. He worked in the fields of easel painting, graphics, monumental and decorative painting, and sculpture, and was fluent in watercolor, etching, pencil and pen drawing. He is the author of more than a thousand works of art (more than 160 have been lost).

The name of Taras Shevchenko is known all over the world: monuments to him have been erected in many countries, his works have been translated into almost every language, and educational institutions, theaters, squares, streets, and cities in Ukraine are named after him. The National Opera of Ukraine, the Kyiv National University, and the central boulevard of Kyiv are named after the outstanding son of the Ukrainian people, Taras Shevchenko.
