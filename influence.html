Influence

Taras Shevchenko's place in Ukrainian society and in Ukrainian culture is special. In Bolshevik times, he was the official symbol of Soviet Ukraine. Today it is the official symbol of a new, independent Ukraine. Back then, he was represented in granite as a figure of an angry, gloomy old descendant of the Haidamaks. Today, a new canonical image of a sufferer, a prophet, a saint is being developed. (Source)

The ideas of national liberation struggle, state independence, and a call for decisive action against violence are a red thread that runs through most of Shevchenko's works. He dispelled the illusions of the serfs about the “good tsar,” angrily criticized Peter I, Catherine II, and his contemporary Nicholas I. His “Dream,” “Caucasus,” “To the Dead and the Living...” and other works called for the overthrow of the autocratic serfdom. All Ukrainians know the famous “Testament” from their school desks, where the poet calls: “...rise up, break the chains and sprinkle the freedom with the enemy's evil blood.” (Source)

Taras Hryhorovych made a huge contribution to the awakening and formation of the national consciousness of the Ukrainian people, to the development of Ukrainian and world culture. The ideas, thoughts, and all the work of Shevchenko's genius have become a living phenomenon that continues to develop in the consciousness of Ukrainian society. How relevant Shevchenko's immortal words sound today: (Source)

“Love your Ukraine, love it... in times of trouble, in the last hard moment, pray to the Lord for it.”
